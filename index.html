Overview

Why Infrastructure Is the New Frontier

Artificial intelligence doesn't exist in the cloud - it runs on physical infrastructure: racks of GPUs, kilometers of fiber, tanks of cooling fluid, and megawatts of electric power. The AI boom of the 2020s has exposed a hard constraint: the world is running out of the infrastructure needed to train and serve the next generation of models.

Israel has responded with one of the densest concentrations of AI infrastructure startups globally. This ecosystem map documents 50 Israeli companies tackling every layer of the stack - from power generation and liquid cooling to photonic interconnects, AI accelerators, and intelligent storage systems. Together, they have raised over $4.5B, built 2 unicorns, and recorded 3 exits. As Grove Ventures sees it: whoever controls the infrastructure controls the future of AI - and Israeli founders are competing to build it.

For Founders & Investors

The Opportunity Ahead

The AI infrastructure build-out is early. The next decade will require orders of magnitude more compute, power, and connectivity - creating generational company-building opportunities.

01
Cooling is Critically Underfunded
Only $50M raised across 8 companies - yet cooling is one of the hardest physical constraints on data center density. With GPU thermal output rising sharply, immersion and liquid cooling are becoming mandatory infrastructure.
02
Orchestration is the Next Wave
With only $51M raised across 7 companies, orchestration & security is underfunded relative to its strategic importance. As AI clusters scale, intelligent management software becomes the critical differentiator.
03
Connectivity Goes Photonic
With $1.3B deployed across 8 companies, the shift from copper to silicon photonics in data centers is well underway. AI clusters requiring massive interconnect bandwidth are driving this transformation.
04
Storage Creates Unicorns
Both Israeli unicorns emerged from storage. VAST Data ($9.1B) and WEKA ($1.6B) prove that AI-native storage capable of feeding GPU clusters at high speed creates extraordinary, durable value.
05
Energy Is an Existential Challenge
11 Israeli companies are tackling AI data center power demands. With 3 already exited and $928M raised, energy is proven ground - and the problem is only growing.
06
Israel's Deep Tech Moat
Decades of excellence in signal processing, photonics, chip design, and systems architecture have created an irreplaceable talent pool. The founders in this ecosystem bring hardware depth that few startup ecosystems replicate.
